Landmark Article
Palivizumab, a Humanized RSV Monoclonal Antibody, Reduces Hospitalization (IMpact-RSV Trial)
The IMpact-RSV Study Group
Pediatrics, 1998 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The IMpact trial proved that passive immunization with palivizumab reduces RSV hospitalizations by 55% in high-risk infants. For 25 years it was the only RSV prevention—now complemented by nirsevimab.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
1,502 high-risk infants (premature or with BPD) randomized to monthly palivizumab or placebo through RSV season.
2
RSV hospitalization reduced by 55% (4.8% vs. 10.6%).
3
Greatest benefit in premature infants without BPD: 78% reduction in hospitalization.
4
RSV is the most common cause of bronchiolitis and pneumonia in infants under 1 year.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the most common cause of bronchiolitis in infants?’ — Respiratory syncytial virus (RSV).
