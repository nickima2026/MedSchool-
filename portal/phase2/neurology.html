Landmark Article
Tissue Plasminogen Activator for Acute Ischemic Stroke (NINDS rt-PA Trial)
The NINDS rt-PA Stroke Study Group
New England Journal of Medicine, 1995 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The definitive trial proving IV tPA works for acute ischemic stroke. Made stroke a time-critical emergency and established the 3-hour treatment window. Every neurology attending will pimp students on this.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
624 patients randomized; tPA group was 30% more likely to have minimal or no disability at 3 months.
2
Time window: Treatment must begin within 3 hours of symptom onset (later extended to 4.5 hours by ECASS III).
3
Risk: Symptomatic ICH in 6.4% of tPA patients vs. 0.6% placebo—the critical risk-benefit discussion.
4
Door-to-needle time goal: <60 minutes. Every minute counts—‘Time is brain.’
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the dose of IV tPA for stroke?’ — 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg); 10% as bolus, remainder over 60 minutes.
