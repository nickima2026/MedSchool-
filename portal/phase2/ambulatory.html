Landmark Article
The Chronic Care Model: Improving Chronic Illness Care
Wagner EH
Effective Clinical Practice, 1998 | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Ed Wagner’s Chronic Care Model transformed ambulatory medicine by shifting from reactive, acute-care visits to proactive, planned chronic disease management. The framework underlies patient-centered medical homes and modern primary care redesign.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Six elements: community resources, health system organization, self-management support, delivery system design, decision support, clinical information systems.
2
Productive interactions between a prepared practice team and an informed, activated patient are key to better outcomes.
3
Self-management support: Patients manage their own disease between visits; the team provides tools and follow-up.
4
Clinical information systems (registries) enable population health management—tracking who needs what, when.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘Name three components of the Chronic Care Model.’ — Self-management support, delivery system design, decision support (among others).
