Landmark Article
ACR Appropriateness Criteria: Evidence-Based Guidelines for Imaging
American College of Radiology
Journal of the American College of Radiology, 2020 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The ACR Appropriateness Criteria are the gold standard for evidence-based imaging decisions. Knowing when to order (and when NOT to order) imaging studies is a core competency—and a favorite pimp topic.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Imaging studies are rated 1–9: 1–3 usually not appropriate, 4–6 may be appropriate, 7–9 usually appropriate.
2
CT Pulmonary Angiography is the gold standard for diagnosing pulmonary embolism (sensitivity ~95%, specificity ~97%).
3
Unnecessary imaging exposes patients to radiation and incidental findings that generate anxiety and further testing.
4
Clinical decision support (CDS) integrated into EHR ordering systems is now required by CMS.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the first-line imaging study for suspected PE?’ — CT pulmonary angiography.
