Landmark Article
A Surgical Safety Checklist to Reduce Morbidity and Mortality in a Global Population
Haynes AB, Weiser TG, Berry WR, et al. (WHO Safe Surgery Saves Lives)
New England Journal of Medicine, 2009 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The WHO Surgical Safety Checklist reduced surgical complications by 36% and deaths by 47% across 8 hospitals in 8 countries. Now used in every operating room worldwide. Atul Gawande’s team proved that a simple checklist saves lives.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
19-item checklist with 3 phases: Sign In (before anesthesia), Time Out (before incision), Sign Out (before leaving OR).
2
Complications: 11.0% → 7.0% (36% reduction). Deaths: 1.5% → 0.8% (47% reduction).
3
Team communication: The Time Out ensures the entire team confirms patient identity, procedure, site, allergies, and anticipated complications.
4
Works across all resource settings—from high-income to low-income hospitals.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What are the three phases of the WHO Surgical Safety Checklist?’ — Sign In, Time Out, Sign Out.
