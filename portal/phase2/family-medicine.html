Landmark Article
The Patient-Centered Medical Home: A Systematic Review
Jackson GL, Powers BJ, Chatterjee R, et al.
Annals of Internal Medicine, 2013 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The patient-centered medical home (PCMH) model defines modern family medicine practice. This systematic review evaluated the evidence supporting PCMH principles—comprehensive, coordinated, accessible, quality-driven primary care.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
PCMH principles: Comprehensive first-contact care, patient-centered, coordinated, accessible, committed to quality and safety.
2
Evidence supports improvements in preventive care delivery and patient experience.
3
Care coordination reduces ED visits and hospitalizations for chronic conditions like diabetes and CHF.
4
Team-based care: Physicians, NPs, PAs, nurses, social workers, and pharmacists working as an integrated team.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What are the principles of the patient-centered medical home?’ — Comprehensive, patient-centered, coordinated, accessible, quality-focused.
