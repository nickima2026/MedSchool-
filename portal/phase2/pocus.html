Landmark Article
Focused Assessment with Sonography for Trauma (FAST): Results from an International Consensus Conference
Scalea TM, Rodriguez A, Chiu WC, et al.
Journal of Trauma, 1999 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The FAST exam is the foundational point-of-care ultrasound protocol and often the first ultrasound skill taught to medical students. It can identify free fluid in trauma patients within minutes—guiding the decision to operate or observe.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Four views: Right upper quadrant (Morrison’s pouch), Left upper quadrant (splenorenal), Subxiphoid (pericardial), Suprapubic (pelvis).
2
Detects free fluid—a surrogate for hemorrhage in the traumatically injured patient.
3
Sensitivity ~85–96% for significant hemoperitoneum; specificity >95%.
4
E-FAST adds bilateral anterior chest views for pneumothorax detection.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘Name the four views of the FAST exam.’ — RUQ, LUQ, subxiphoid, suprapubic.
