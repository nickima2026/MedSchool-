Landmark Article
Practice Guidelines for Management of the Difficult Airway
American Society of Anesthesiologists Task Force
Anesthesiology, 2022 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The ASA Difficult Airway Algorithm is the most important clinical guideline in anesthesia. It provides a decision tree for managing anticipated and unanticipated difficult airways—situations where failure means death.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Preoperative airway assessment: Mallampati score, thyromental distance, neck mobility, mouth opening predict difficulty.
2
Awake intubation (usually fiberoptic) is the safest approach when a difficult airway is anticipated.
3
Cannot Intubate, Cannot Oxygenate (CICO): Emergency surgical airway (cricothyrotomy) is the final rescue step.
4
Supraglottic airways (LMA) serve as rescue devices when intubation fails but ventilation is possible.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is your plan if you cannot intubate AND cannot ventilate?’ — Surgical cricothyrotomy.
