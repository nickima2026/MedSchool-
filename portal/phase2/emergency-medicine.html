Landmark Article
A Study to Develop Clinical Decision Rules for the Use of Radiography in Acute Ankle Injuries (Ottawa Ankle Rules)
Stiell IG, Greenberg GH, McKnight RD, et al.
Annals of Emergency Medicine, 1992 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The Ottawa Ankle Rules are the most validated clinical decision rule in emergency medicine. They reduced unnecessary ankle X-rays by 30–40% without missing fractures—the gold standard for evidence-based clinical decision rules.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Ankle X-ray indicated only if: pain near malleoli AND (bone tenderness at posterior edge/tip of either malleolus, OR inability to bear weight for 4 steps).
2
Foot X-ray indicated only if: midfoot pain AND (bone tenderness at navicular or base of 5th metatarsal, OR inability to bear weight).
3
Sensitivity: 100% for clinically significant fractures—essentially no missed fractures.
4
Validated in over 40 studies across multiple countries and patient populations.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘When does an ankle injury NOT need an X-ray?’ — When the Ottawa Ankle Rules are negative (no bone tenderness, can bear weight).
