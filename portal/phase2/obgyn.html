Landmark Article
Do Women with Pre-eclampsia, and Their Babies, Benefit from Magnesium Sulphate? (The Magpie Trial)
The Magpie Trial Collaborative Group
The Lancet, 2002 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The largest RCT in pre-eclampsia history (10,141 women across 33 countries). Proved that magnesium sulfate halves the risk of eclampsia—making it the standard of care worldwide.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
10,141 women with pre-eclampsia randomized to MgSO4 or placebo across 33 countries.
2
MgSO4 reduced eclampsia risk by 58% (0.8% vs. 1.9%; NNT = 91).
3
Maternal mortality was also lower with MgSO4 (RR 0.55), though not statistically significant.
4
Side effects: 24% reported flushing/warmth—but no serious harm to mother or baby.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the treatment for eclampsia prophylaxis in pre-eclampsia?’ — Magnesium sulfate.
