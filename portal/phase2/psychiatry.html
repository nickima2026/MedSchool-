Landmark Article
Sequenced Treatment Alternatives to Relieve Depression (STAR*D): Study Design
Rush AJ, Fava M, Wisniewski SR, et al.
American Journal of Psychiatry, 2006 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The largest real-world depression treatment trial ever conducted (4,041 patients). Showed that ~1/3 of patients remit with first-line SSRI, but cumulative remission reaches ~67% by the fourth treatment step—if patients persist through sequential trials.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Level 1: Citalopram alone achieved ~28% remission rate. Remission (not just response) was the goal.
2
Level 2: Switch or augment—options included bupropion, sertraline, venlafaxine, CBT, or augmentation with bupropion/buspirone.
3
Cumulative remission: ~67% of patients eventually remitted across all four levels, but relapse rates increased with each subsequent step.
4
Key lesson: Depression often requires multiple sequential treatment trials; don’t give up after one medication fails.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What was the remission rate with first-line SSRI in STAR*D?’ — Approximately 28%.
