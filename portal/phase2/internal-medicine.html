Landmark Article
Evidence Based Medicine: What It Is and What It Isn’t
Sackett DL, Rosenberg WMC, Gray JAM, Haynes RB, Richardson WS
BMJ, 1996 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
David Sackett defined evidence-based medicine (EBM) and defended it against early criticism. His definition—integrating clinical expertise, patient values, and best available evidence—is the operating framework of all modern internal medicine practice.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
EBM integrates: (1) best available research evidence, (2) clinical expertise, (3) patient values and preferences.
2
EBM is not ‘cookbook medicine’—clinical expertise is required to apply evidence to individual patients.
3
Hierarchy of evidence: Systematic reviews > RCTs > cohort studies > case series > expert opinion.
4
Without clinical expertise, practice risks being tyrannized by evidence; without evidence, practice risks being out of date.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What are the three pillars of evidence-based medicine?’ — Best evidence, clinical expertise, patient values.
