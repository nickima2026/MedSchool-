Landmark Article
Early Goal-Directed Therapy in Severe Sepsis and Septic Shock
Rivers E, Nguyen B, Havstad S, et al.
New England Journal of Medicine, 2001 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Rivers’ landmark trial demonstrated that protocolized early resuscitation in the first 6 hours of severe sepsis/septic shock reduced in-hospital mortality from 46.5% to 30.5%. This paper launched the Surviving Sepsis Campaign and remains the most-cited article in critical care.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
EGDT protocol: Target CVP 8–12 mmHg, MAP ≥65 mmHg, urine output ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr, ScvO2 ≥70%.
2
Mortality reduction: 30.5% vs. 46.5%—a 16% absolute reduction with early, aggressive resuscitation.
3
Sepsis bundles: 3-hour and 6-hour bundles derived from this evidence (lactate, blood cultures, antibiotics, fluids).
4
Later trials (ProCESS, ARISE, ProMISe) showed similar mortality across protocols—but all groups received early care, validating the concept.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the Surviving Sepsis 1-hour bundle?’ — Measure lactate, blood cultures, broad-spectrum antibiotics, 30 mL/kg crystalloid if hypotensive, vasopressors if MAP <65.
