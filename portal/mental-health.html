Mental Health & Wellness

Medical school is one of the most demanding journeys you'll ever take. The stress, doubt, and exhaustion you feel are not signs of weakness — they're signs that you care deeply about becoming an excellent physician.

Every medical student experiences mental health stress during training. You are not alone. The important thing is to find healthy ways to cope, ask for help when you need it, and remember that struggling is part of growing.
🚨
In crisis right now? If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line). You can also call the Physician Support Line: 1-888-409-0141 (Mon–Fri, 8 AM – 12 AM ET). These services are free and confidential.

Healthy Coping Strategies

Small, evidence-based habits that build resilience over time.

🚶

Move Your Body

Even 10 minutes of walking reduces cortisol levels. Physical activity is one of the most studied interventions for depression and anxiety.

💤

Protect Your Sleep

Sleep deprivation impairs clinical reasoning, memory consolidation, and emotional regulation. Prioritize 7+ hours when possible.

👥

Stay Connected

Isolation amplifies stress. Even brief social contact — a coffee with a classmate, a call home — buffers against burnout.

📓

Journal or Reflect

Writing about stressful experiences for just 15 minutes has been shown to reduce intrusive thoughts and improve working memory.

🎨

Creative Outlets

Art, music, cooking, or any creative activity activates the default mode network and provides cognitive rest from medical study.

🛑

Set Boundaries

Learning to say no and carving out protected personal time is not selfish — it's necessary for sustainable performance.

🌿

Spend Time Outdoors

Nature exposure reduces rumination and lowers activity in the brain's self-referential worry circuits. Even campus green spaces help.

🧑‍⚕️

See a Professional

Therapy is a strength. Cognitive behavioral therapy has strong evidence for anxiety, depression, and imposter syndrome common in medical training.
