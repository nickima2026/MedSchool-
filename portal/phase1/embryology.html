Landmark Article
Hedgehog Signaling in Animal Development and Disease
Ingham PW, McMahon AP
Genes & Development, 2001 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Sonic Hedgehog (Shh) signaling is one of the most critical pathways in embryonic development. This review established the framework for understanding how Shh patterns the neural tube, limbs, and gut—and how its disruption causes congenital malformations like holoprosencephaly.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Sonic Hedgehog (Shh) acts as a morphogen: concentration gradients specify different cell fates in the developing neural tube.
2
Ventral neural tube patterning: Shh from the notochord and floor plate specifies motor neurons and ventral interneurons.
3
Limb development: Shh from the zone of polarizing activity (ZPA) establishes anterior-posterior axis of digits.
4
Holoprosencephaly: The most common forebrain malformation; often linked to defects in Shh signaling.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What structure secretes Sonic Hedgehog to pattern the neural tube?’ — The notochord.
