Landmark Article
Engel’s Biopsychosocial Model: The Need for a New Medical Model
Engel GL
Science, 1977 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
George Engel argued that the biomedical model alone was insufficient—physicians must consider biological, psychological, and social dimensions of illness. This paper established the patient-centered approach that defines modern medical practice.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Biomedical model is reductionist: treats disease as purely biological, ignoring psychological and social factors.
2
Biopsychosocial model: Illness results from the interplay of biological, psychological, and social factors.
3
A patient’s experience of disease is shaped by beliefs, coping skills, social support, and cultural context.
4
The physician-patient relationship itself is therapeutic—active listening and empathy improve outcomes.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the biopsychosocial model?’ — Illness involves biological, psychological, and social dimensions (Engel, 1977).
