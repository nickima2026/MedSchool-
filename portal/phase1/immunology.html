Landmark Article
Approaching the Asymptote? Evolution and Revolution in Immunology
Janeway CA Jr.
Cold Spring Harbor Symposia on Quantitative Biology, 1989 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Charles Janeway’s visionary paper predicted the existence of pattern recognition receptors (PRRs) that detect pathogen-associated molecular patterns (PAMPs)—a decade before Toll-like receptors were discovered. This paper bridged the gap between innate and adaptive immunity.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Proposed that the immune system has evolved receptors to recognize conserved microbial structures (PAMPs).
2
Predicted pattern recognition receptors (PRRs)—later confirmed as Toll-like receptors (TLRs), validated by the 2011 Nobel Prize.
3
Innate immunity is not ‘non-specific’—it recognizes specific molecular patterns shared across pathogen classes.
4
Adjuvants work by activating innate immune receptors, bridging innate detection to adaptive immune responses.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What are PAMPs?’ — Pathogen-associated molecular patterns; conserved microbial structures recognized by PRRs like TLRs.
