Landmark Article
Half a Century Legacy of Long-Term Potentiation
Caya-Bissonnette L, Béïque JC
Current Biology, 2024 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Reviews the landmark 1973 discovery by Bliss and Lømo that high-frequency stimulation produces long-lasting synaptic strengthening (LTP) in the hippocampus—the leading cellular model for learning and memory.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Long-term potentiation (LTP): Persistent strengthening of synapses based on patterns of activity—the cellular basis of memory.
2
NMDA receptors act as coincidence detectors: require both presynaptic glutamate release AND postsynaptic depolarization.
3
Early LTP requires kinase activation (CaMKII); late LTP requires new protein synthesis and gene transcription.
4
Hippocampus is critical for declarative memory formation; LTP is most extensively studied in hippocampal CA1.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What receptor is required for LTP induction?’ — The NMDA receptor.
