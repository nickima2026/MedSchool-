Landmark Article
Initial Sequencing and Analysis of the Human Genome
Lander ES, Linton LM, Birren B, et al. (International Human Genome Sequencing Consortium)
Nature, 2001 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The Human Genome Project completion paper—one of the greatest scientific achievements in history. Revealed that humans have ~20,000–25,000 genes (far fewer than expected), and that >50% of the genome consists of repeat sequences. Launched the era of genomic medicine.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Human genome contains approximately 3.2 billion base pairs and ~20,000–25,000 protein-coding genes.
2
Only ~1.5% of the genome codes for protein; the rest includes regulatory elements, introns, and repeat sequences.
3
Gene density varies by chromosome: chromosome 19 is gene-rich; chromosome 13 is gene-poor.
4
~99.9% of DNA sequence is identical between any two humans; the 0.1% variation includes SNPs that determine disease risk.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘How many protein-coding genes are in the human genome?’ — Approximately 20,000.
