Landmark Article
Molecular Structure of Nucleic Acids: A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid
Watson JD, Crick FHC
Nature, 1953 | DOI
Why This Paper Matters
The one-page paper that changed biology forever. Watson and Crick’s description of the DNA double helix explained how genetic information is stored and replicated, launching the entire field of molecular biology.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
DNA is a double helix with antiparallel strands connected by complementary base pairing (A-T, G-C).
2
The structure immediately suggests a replication mechanism: each strand serves as a template for a new complementary strand.
3
Base pairing rules (Chargaff’s rules): A=T and G=C in quantity; this was the key insight enabling the structure.
4
The major and minor grooves of the helix provide binding sites for regulatory proteins and transcription factors.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the central dogma of molecular biology?’ — DNA → RNA → Protein.
