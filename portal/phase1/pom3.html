Landmark Article
An Objective Structured Clinical Examination (OSCE) for Medical Education
Harden RM, Stevenson M, Downie WW, Wilson GM
British Medical Journal, 1975 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Introduced the OSCE—now the global standard for assessing clinical competence. Every medical student takes an OSCE; understanding its design helps you perform better.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
OSCE uses standardized patients and structured scoring to objectively assess clinical skills.
2
Stations test history-taking, physical examination, communication, and procedural skills in a timed format.
3
Reliability comes from standardization: every student faces the same scenarios with the same scoring rubrics.
4
OSCEs assess competencies that written exams cannot: empathy, professionalism, and clinical reasoning under pressure.
★
