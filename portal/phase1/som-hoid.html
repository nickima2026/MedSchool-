Landmark Article
Early Goal-Directed Therapy in the Treatment of Severe Sepsis and Septic Shock
Rivers E, Nguyen B, Havstad S, et al.
New England Journal of Medicine, 2001 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The Rivers trial transformed sepsis management by demonstrating that protocolized early resuscitation in the ED—before ICU admission—reduced mortality from 46.5% to 30.5%. Launched the era of early, aggressive sepsis treatment and the Surviving Sepsis Campaign.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
263 patients with severe sepsis/septic shock randomized to 6 hours of early goal-directed therapy (EGDT) vs. standard care.
2
EGDT protocol targeted CVP 8–12, MAP ≥65, urine output ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr, and ScvO2 ≥70%.
3
In-hospital mortality: 30.5% (EGDT) vs. 46.5% (standard)—absolute reduction of 16%.
4
The ‘golden hours’ concept: Early recognition and resuscitation are the most important determinants of sepsis survival.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What were the targets of early goal-directed therapy for sepsis?’ — CVP 8–12, MAP ≥65, UOP ≥0.5, ScvO2 ≥70%.
