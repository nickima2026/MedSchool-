Landmark Article
Part 3: Adult Basic and Advanced Life Support — 2020 AHA Guidelines
Panchal AR, Bartos JA, Cabañas JG, et al.
Circulation, 2020 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The definitive evidence-based guidelines for CPR and emergency cardiovascular care. Defines compression depth, rate, and the chain of survival that every healthcare provider must know.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
High-quality chest compressions: Rate 100–120/min, depth at least 2 inches (5 cm), full recoil, minimal interruptions.
2
Compression-to-ventilation ratio: 30:2 for single rescuer; continuous compressions with advanced airway.
3
Early defibrillation: Every minute without defibrillation decreases survival by 7–10%.
4
Chain of Survival: Recognition → Call 911 → CPR → Defibrillation → Advanced care → Recovery.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the compression rate for adult CPR?’ — 100–120 compressions per minute.
