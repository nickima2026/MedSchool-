Landmark Article
Shared Decision Making—The Pinnacle of Patient-Centered Care
Barry MJ, Edgman-Levitan S
New England Journal of Medicine, 2012 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Defined shared decision making as the standard for clinical encounters where multiple reasonable options exist. Attendings expect students to articulate this framework during patient care discussions.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Shared decision making integrates the clinician’s expertise with the patient’s values and preferences.
2
Especially critical for ‘preference-sensitive’ decisions: PSA screening, knee replacement timing, end-of-life care.
3
Decision aids improve knowledge, reduce decisional conflict, and increase patient engagement.
4
Three-step model: (1) Recognize a decision exists, (2) Describe options with risks/benefits, (3) Help patient deliberate based on their values.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘When is shared decision making most important?’ — When there are multiple medically reasonable options and the choice depends on patient values.
