Landmark Article
The Framingham Heart Study: Pioneering Cardiovascular Epidemiology
Dawber TR, Meadors GF, Moore FE
American Journal of Public Health, 1951 | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Launched in 1948 with 5,209 residents of Framingham, Massachusetts, this ongoing prospective cohort study established the concept of ‘risk factors’ for cardiovascular disease. It identified hypertension, high cholesterol, smoking, obesity, and diabetes as modifiable risks—shaping all of modern preventive cardiology.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Prospective cohort design: Followed healthy subjects over decades to identify what predicts disease—the model for modern epidemiologic studies.
2
Coined ‘risk factor’: The term was popularized through Framingham publications.
3
Key findings: Hypertension, hyperlipidemia, smoking, diabetes, and obesity identified as independent cardiovascular risk factors.
4
Framingham Risk Score: Derived from this cohort; still used clinically to estimate 10-year cardiovascular risk.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What type of study design is Framingham?’ — Prospective (longitudinal) cohort study.
