Landmark Article
Bad Bugs, No Drugs: No ESKAPE!
Boucher HW, Talbot GH, Bradley JS, et al.
Clinical Infectious Diseases, 2009 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Defined the ESKAPE pathogens—the six most dangerous antibiotic-resistant organisms—and sounded the alarm on the antibiotic pipeline crisis. Every medical student on an ID rotation will encounter these organisms.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
ESKAPE pathogens: Enterococcus faecium, Staphylococcus aureus, Klebsiella pneumoniae, Acinetobacter baumannii, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Enterobacter species.
2
These organisms ‘escape’ the effects of most available antibiotics through resistance mechanisms.
3
Antibiotic pipeline: Far fewer new antibiotics in development compared to the 1980s–1990s; the crisis continues.
4
MRSA, VRE, and carbapenem-resistant Enterobacteriaceae (CRE) are the most clinically urgent threats.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘Name the ESKAPE pathogens.’ — Know the mnemonic: E-S-K-A-P-E.
