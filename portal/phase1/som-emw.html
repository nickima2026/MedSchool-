Landmark Article
Diabetes Control and Complications Trial (DCCT)
The DCCT Research Group
New England Journal of Medicine, 1993 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The landmark trial proving that tight glucose control prevents microvascular complications in Type 1 diabetes. Showed intensive therapy (HbA1c ~7%) reduced retinopathy by 76%, nephropathy by 50%, and neuropathy by 60% compared to conventional therapy.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
1,441 patients with Type 1 diabetes randomized to intensive (3+ insulin injections/day or pump) vs. conventional therapy.
2
Intensive therapy achieved HbA1c ~7% vs. ~9% in conventional group.
3
Retinopathy risk reduced 76%; nephropathy 50%; neuropathy 60% with intensive therapy.
4
Major adverse effect: 3-fold increase in severe hypoglycemia in the intensive group.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What did the DCCT prove?’ — Intensive insulin therapy significantly reduces microvascular complications in Type 1 diabetes.
