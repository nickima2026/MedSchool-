Landmark Article
Teaching Anatomy: A Practical Guide (Anatomical Education Landmark)
Drake RL, McBride JM, Lachman N, Pawlina W
Anatomical Sciences Education, 2009 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Addressed the crisis in anatomy education as dissection time decreased across medical schools. Advocated for clinical correlation in anatomy teaching—the approach now standard in all modern curricula including Stanford’s.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Clinical anatomy integrates anatomical knowledge with clinical scenarios—the ‘why’ behind the structures.
2
Cadaveric dissection remains the gold standard but must be supplemented with imaging, simulation, and clinical cases.
3
Surface anatomy and living anatomy are increasingly important for physical examination skills.
4
Anatomical variants are common (e.g., aberrant right subclavian artery in ~1% of population)—surgeons must anticipate them.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What nerve runs through the carpal tunnel?’ — The median nerve.
