Landmark Article
Medical Education in the United States and Canada (The Flexner Report)
Flexner A
Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching, 1910
Why This Paper Matters
Abraham Flexner’s 1910 report closed half the medical schools in North America and established the modern standards: 2 years of basic science + 2 years of clinical training, university affiliation, and scientific rigor. Every medical school you attend exists because of this report.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Surveyed all 155 medical schools in the US and Canada; recommended closing the majority for inadequate standards.
2
Established the ‘2+2’ model: 2 years preclinical basic sciences, 2 years clinical clerkships—still the standard today.
3
Required university affiliation, full-time faculty, and teaching hospitals for clinical training.
4
Criticized: Over-emphasized biomedical science at the expense of social determinants, rural medicine, and diversity.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is the Flexner Report?’ — The 1910 Carnegie Foundation report that established modern medical education standards in North America.
