Landmark Article
Social Determinants of Health: The Solid Facts (Marmot Report)
Marmot M, Wilkinson RG (editors)
World Health Organization (Europe), 2003
Why This Paper Matters
Michael Marmot’s work demonstrated that health follows a social gradient—the lower your socioeconomic position, the worse your health. This framework underlies all modern community health, public health policy, and health equity work.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
The social gradient: Health improves at each step up the socioeconomic ladder—it’s not just a rich vs. poor divide.
2
Ten key social determinants: stress, early life, social exclusion, work, unemployment, social support, addiction, food, transport, and the gradient itself.
3
Chronic psychosocial stress activates the HPA axis and increases cortisol—driving cardiovascular disease, immune suppression, and mental illness.
4
Health inequity is avoidable and unjust—interventions must address upstream social conditions, not just downstream disease.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What are social determinants of health?’ — Conditions in which people are born, grow, live, work, and age that shape health outcomes.
