Landmark Article
High-Performance Medicine: The Convergence of Human and Artificial Intelligence
Topol EJ
Nature Medicine, 2019 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
Eric Topol’s landmark review of AI in medicine—covering deep learning for imaging, pathology, genomics, and clinical decision support. Defines the opportunities and limitations that every physician entering the AI era must understand.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Deep learning matches or exceeds specialist performance in radiology (chest X-ray, mammography), pathology (metastasis detection), and dermatology (melanoma).
2
AI will augment, not replace, physicians—the goal is to restore the human connection by reducing clerical burden.
3
Limitations: Black-box models lack explainability; biased training data perpetuates health disparities.
4
Prospective validation and regulatory frameworks are essential before clinical deployment.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What are the main risks of deploying AI in clinical medicine?’ — Bias in training data, lack of explainability, and insufficient prospective validation.
