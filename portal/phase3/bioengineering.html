Landmark Article
Tissue Engineering
Langer R, Vacanti JP
Science, 1993 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
The paper that founded the field of tissue engineering. Langer and Vacanti proposed combining cells, scaffolds, and growth factors to create functional tissue replacements—a vision now being realized in skin grafts, cartilage repair, and organ-on-chip technology.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Tissue engineering combines three elements: cells, scaffolds (biodegradable polymers), and signaling molecules (growth factors).
2
Scaffolds provide a 3D template for cells to attach, proliferate, and differentiate into functional tissue.
3
Current clinical applications: skin substitutes for burns, cartilage repair, and bladder augmentation.
4
Future frontiers: Bioprinted organs, organ-on-chip drug testing, and decellularized organ scaffolds for transplant.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What are the three pillars of tissue engineering?’ — Cells, scaffolds, and signaling molecules.
