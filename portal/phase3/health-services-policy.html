Landmark Article
To Err Is Human: Building a Safer Health System
Kohn LT, Corrigan JM, Donaldson MS (Institute of Medicine)
National Academies Press, 1999
Why This Paper Matters
The IOM report that shocked healthcare: estimated 44,000–98,000 Americans die annually from preventable medical errors—more than car accidents, breast cancer, or AIDS. Launched the modern patient safety movement.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Medical errors kill 44,000–98,000 Americans per year—making them a leading cause of death.
2
Most errors are system failures, not individual incompetence: poorly designed processes, not bad people.
3
Swiss cheese model: Errors occur when multiple system safeguards fail simultaneously.
4
Recommendations: Mandatory error reporting, non-punitive culture, standardized protocols, and technology solutions.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘How many deaths per year are attributed to medical errors in the US?’ — 44,000–98,000 (IOM, 1999); more recent estimates suggest >250,000.
