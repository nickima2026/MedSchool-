Landmark Article
The CONSORT Statement: Revised Recommendations for Improving the Quality of Reports of Parallel-Group Randomized Trials
Moher D, Schulz KF, Altman DG (CONSORT Group)
Annals of Internal Medicine, 2001 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
CONSORT (Consolidated Standards of Reporting Trials) established the minimum reporting requirements for randomized controlled trials. Used by virtually every major medical journal—if you’re going to do clinical research, you must know CONSORT.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
22-item checklist covering title, methods, randomization, blinding, outcomes, flow diagram, and interpretation.
2
The CONSORT flow diagram tracks participants from enrollment through allocation, follow-up, and analysis.
3
Intention-to-treat analysis: Participants are analyzed in the group to which they were randomized, regardless of compliance.
4
Over 600 journals endorse CONSORT; it is now the global standard for RCT reporting.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘What is intention-to-treat analysis?’ — Analyzing patients in their originally randomized group regardless of whether they completed the protocol.
