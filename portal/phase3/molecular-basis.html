Landmark Article
The Hallmarks of Cancer
Hanahan D, Weinberg RA
Cell, 2000 | DOI | PubMed
Why This Paper Matters
One of the most cited papers in biomedical science. Hanahan and Weinberg distilled the complexity of cancer into six (later expanded to ten) acquired capabilities that all cancers share—the organizing framework for all of molecular oncology.
Key Takeaways for Rounds
1
Original 6 hallmarks: (1) Self-sufficiency in growth signals, (2) Insensitivity to anti-growth signals, (3) Evading apoptosis, (4) Limitless replicative potential, (5) Sustained angiogenesis, (6) Tissue invasion and metastasis.
2
2011 update added: Reprogramming energy metabolism, Evading immune destruction, Genome instability, Tumor-promoting inflammation.
3
Oncogenes (gain-of-function) and tumor suppressors (loss-of-function) drive these hallmarks.
4
Multi-hit hypothesis: Cancer requires accumulation of multiple genetic alterations—explains why cancer incidence increases with age.
★
Pimp Pearl: ‘Name the six original hallmarks of cancer.’ — Know all six. Attendings love this question.
